Why It Matters

The Digital Carbon Crisis

Your phone and laptop feel weightless — but the data they generate has a very real physical footprint.

🏭

Data Centers = Factories

Global data centers consume over 200 TWh of electricity per year — comparable to the annual electricity use of some medium-sized countries. Most of that energy goes to servers holding data nobody accesses.

💧

Water: The Hidden Cost

Data centers use billions of gallons of water annually just to cool their servers. One average data center can consume as much water as a hospital or a small manufacturing plant. Cooling unused files is a literal waste of a precious resource.

📧

The Email Problem

Sending or receiving a single email emits 4 grams of CO₂ — up to 50 g with large attachments. With 300+ billion emails sent every day, our inboxes are one of the planet's most overlooked pollution sources.

📸

Duplicate Photos

The average smartphone user has 3× more photos than they need — burst shots, near-identical frames, blurry takes. Auto-uploading these to the cloud multiplies their carbon cost many times over, often across multiple backup services simultaneously.

🌿

Your Action Scales

When millions of people delete even 5 GB each, the aggregate saving is enormous. ByteSaver's leaderboard turns individual micro-actions into a visible, collective wave of climate progress.

🔢

The Math Is Simple

1 GB deleted → 0.25 kg CO₂ saved/year.
1 kg CO₂ = 4 km not driven 🚗
1 kg CO₂ = 120 phones not charged 📱
20 kg CO₂ = 1 tree's full year of work 🌳
